A gaming laptop under $1,000 in 2026 means setting expectations correctly: you are buying an RTX 5050 or RTX 5060, a 1080p IPS panel and a plastic chassis, usually with at least one corner cut on the display or cooling. The good news is that this generation's budget GPUs handle 1080p comfortably, and most of these machines let you upgrade RAM and storage yourself.

The Lenovo LOQ 15 is our favourite here — it pairs the RTX 5060 with a 144 Hz panel and regularly dips under the cap on sale. The MSI Katana 15 HX is the surprise, fitting a full RTX 5070 into this bracket, while the HP Victus 15 is the rock-bottom option. The MSI Cyborg 15 and Acer Nitro V 16 AI round out the field for buyers who value light weight and genuine serviceability respectively.

How we chose: every pick lands at or near the $1,000 cap, often through sale pricing, and we prioritised models you can upgrade over sealed ones.

The big caveat: this is where corners get cut. Several of these ship single-channel RAM that throttles gaming until you add a second stick, and the Victus has documented hinge and cooling complaints. Buy with upgrades in mind, and treat the cheapest models as semi-disposable.
