Under $1,500 is arguably the best-value bracket in gaming laptops: it is where the RTX 5070 becomes attainable and where you stop having to choose between a decent screen and a decent GPU. Most picks here are also genuinely upgradeable, so they will age better than sealed premium machines.

The ASUS TUF F16 and A16 are the all-rounders, offering an OLED panel option and two-SO-DIMM upgradeability that is rare at this price. The MSI Crosshair 16 HX AI and Katana 15 HX both deliver RTX 5070 power with RAM headroom up to 96 GB, while the Lenovo LOQ 15 and Acer Nitro V 16 AI anchor the value end. The Alienware 16 Aurora brings brand build quality and a big battery, with the caveat of an 80 W GPU cap.

How we chose: every laptop here has a starting price at or below roughly $1,500, and we leaned toward serviceable models with the best panel-and-GPU balance for the money.

Caveats to know: several of these ship single-channel RAM that hurts gaming until you add a second stick, the Crosshair's USB-C port routes through the iGPU (use HDMI for external monitors), and the Alienware 16 Aurora's RTX 5060 is throttled to 80 W. None are dealbreakers if you know about them going in.
