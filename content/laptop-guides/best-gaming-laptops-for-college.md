A gaming laptop for college has to do double duty: game well in the dorm, then survive a backpack, a lecture hall and a library all-nighter. That means weighting portability, battery life and value more heavily than peak frame rates — and ideally choosing something upgradeable so it lasts all four years.

For most students the Lenovo LOQ 15 is the sensible starting point: affordable, capable, and equipped with 100 W USB-C charging so a power bank can keep it alive. If you carry your laptop all day, the 1.5 kg ASUS ROG Zephyrus G14 is the ultimate grab-and-go, while the Lenovo Legion 7a Gen 11 offers the best real-world battery here at roughly 6 to 8 hours of light use. The ASUS TUF A16 and F16 and the Acer Nitro V 16 AI are the value-and-upgradeability picks for a desk-bound setup.

How we chose: we prioritised weight, battery life, charging flexibility and long-term upgradeability over outright GPU power.

A caveat worth flagging: the most portable options here, the Zephyrus G14 and Legion 7a, use soldered RAM, so configure enough memory up front. The roomier TUF and Nitro V models let you add RAM later but weigh more and last less time off the charger — pick the trade-off that fits your campus day.
