Streaming asks more of a laptop than gaming alone: you are running a demanding game and a live encode at the same time, so the CPU and cooling have to sustain a heavy combined load for hours without throttling. That makes multi-core CPU power and thermal headroom the priorities here — peak benchmark numbers matter less than what the machine holds under continuous load.

The ASUS ROG Strix G16 leads with a 24-core CPU and one of the best cooling setups in its class (vapor chamber, Tri-Fan and liquid metal). The Lenovo Legion Pro 7i and MSI Raider 16 Max HX pair 24-core CPUs with serious cooling headroom, while the Gigabyte AORUS Master 16's AMD X3D chip stays remarkably cool under load. The XMG Neo 16 simply does not thermally throttle, and the Lenovo Legion Pro 5 is the value pick with a 16-core, 32-thread Ryzen 9.

How we chose: we weighted multi-core CPU performance and sustained cooling above all else, since those govern a stream's stability.

The trade-off is the familiar one for high-power machines: fan noise. Most of these get loud under combined gaming-and-encoding load — the Raider and AORUS in particular — so a decent external mic with noise suppression is worth budgeting for if your laptop sits near your microphone.
